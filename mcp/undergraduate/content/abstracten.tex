\keywordsen{FastMCP;  Human-Vehicle Interaction;  Simulation Control System;  Natural Language Interaction}
\begin{abstracten}

Aiming at the problems of complex operation, weak natural language
interaction ability and low efficiency of scenario deployment in
traditional human-vehicle interaction simulation systems, this paper
designs and implements an interactive control system for human-vehicle
simulators based on the FastMCP communication framework. Focusing on
human-vehicle interaction behaviors, the system integrates functions
such as simulation environment construction, vehicle control, data
collection and visual display, and realizes lightweight scheduling and
rapid configuration of simulation processes through natural language
instructions. This research first sorts out the research status in the
field of autonomous driving simulation and human-computer interaction,
and clarifies the system design requirements. Secondly, a hierarchical
architecture is constructed to complete the detailed design of
communication module, control module, simulation module and interface
module. Then, the full-process development is realized based on the
CARLA simulation engine and FastMCP protocol, supporting flexible
configuration of multiple scenarios and parameters. Finally, multiple
sets of tests verify the stability and practicability of the system. The
experimental results show that the system can effectively lower the
threshold of simulation operation and improve the efficiency of
human-vehicle interaction testing, providing a convenient, efficient and
scalable development and testing platform for autonomous driving
algorithm verification and human-vehicle interaction behavior analysis.

\end{abstracten}
